# CME Group Fee Schedules and Rebate Products

**Date**: 2025-11-30
**Source**: <https://www.cmegroup.com/markets/products.html>

## Overview

CME Group operates multiple exchanges (CME, CBOT, NYMEX, COMEX) offering futures and options across asset classes including equity indexes, interest rates, energy, metals, agriculture, and foreign exchange. This document outlines fee structures, rebate programs, and cost optimization strategies for trading on CME Group exchanges.

---

## Product Categories

CME Group offers products across six major asset classes:

1. **Equity Indexes**: E-mini S&P 500 (ES), E-mini NASDAQ-100 (NQ), E-mini Dow (YM), E-mini Russell 2000 (RTY)
2. **Interest Rates**: Treasury futures (ZB, ZN, ZF, ZT), Eurodollar futures (GE, ZQ)
3. **Energy**: Crude oil (CL), Natural gas (NG), Heating oil (HO), Gasoline (RB)
4. **Metals**: Gold (GC), Silver (SI), Copper (HG), Platinum (PL)
5. **Agriculture**: Corn (C), Soybeans (S), Wheat (W), Live cattle (LC)
6. **Foreign Exchange**: Euro (6E), Japanese Yen (6J), British Pound (6B), Australian Dollar (6A)

## Rebate Programs

CME Group offers various rebate and incentive programs designed to reward liquidity provision and high-volume trading.

### Volume-Based Rebates

Rebate programs typically structured by:

1. **Monthly Volume Tiers**: Rebates increase with higher monthly contract volume
2. **Product-Specific Rebates**: Different rebate rates for different product categories
3. **Maker-Taker Model**: Different fees/rebates for liquidity providers vs. takers
4. **Time-Based Incentives**: Special rebates during specific trading hours or periods

### Common Rebate Structures

#### Equity Index Products (Example Structure)

| Monthly Volume | Rebate Rate |
|----------------|-------------|
| 0 - 9,999 contracts | Base rate |
| 10,000 - 49,999 | Tier 1 rebate |
| 50,000 - 99,999 | Tier 2 rebate |
| 100,000+ | Tier 3 rebate |

**Note**: Actual rebate rates vary by product and are subject to change. Verify current rates with CME Group.

#### Interest Rate Products

- Typically lower rebate rates than equity indexes
- Volume thresholds may differ
- Product-specific rebate structures

#### Energy Products

- Volume-based rebates
- Product-specific tiers
- May include time-of-day incentives

### Liquidity Provider Incentives

CME Group offers incentives for:

1. **Market Makers**: Rebates for providing continuous two-sided quotes
2. **Electronic Market Makers (EMMs)**: Enhanced rebates for electronic liquidity
3. **Designated Market Makers (DMMs)**: Specialized rebate programs
4. **Cross-Market Rebates**: Incentives for trading across multiple CME Group exchanges

### New Product Incentives

- **Launch Rebates**: Enhanced rebates for new product launches
- **Volume Incentives**: Temporary rebate increases to build liquidity
- **Time-Limited Programs**: Special rebate periods for specific products

## Market Data Fees

CME Group market data pricing includes:

### Real-Time Market Data

- **CME Globex Market Data**: Real-time prices, depth, and trade data
- **CME ClearPort Market Data**: OTC clearing data
- **Historical Data**: End-of-day and tick-by-tick historical data

### Market Data Products

1. **Level 1 Data**: Top-of-book quotes and last trade
2. **Level 2 Data**: Market depth (order book)
3. **Level 3 Data**: Full order book with order-level detail
4. **Historical Data**: DataMine historical data access

### Pricing Structure

Market data fees typically structured as:

- **Per-User Fees**: Monthly fees per user/terminal
- **Enterprise Licenses**: Unlimited user licenses
- **Professional vs. Non-Professional**: Different pricing tiers
- **Redistribution Fees**: Additional fees for redistributing data

**Note**: Market data fees are separate from exchange trading fees. Contact CME Group for current pricing.

## Integration with Box Spread Trading

### Relevance to Box Spread Strategies

While box spreads are primarily options strategies (traded on CBOE, not CME), CME Group products are relevant for:

1. **Hedging**: Using equity index futures to hedge box spread positions
2. **Arbitrage**: Comparing box spread yields to futures financing rates
3. **Capital Efficiency**: Using futures for capital-efficient exposure
4. **Basis Trading**: Trading basis between options and futures

### Cost Considerations

When using CME products alongside box spreads:

1. **Total Cost Analysis**: Include CME fees when calculating total strategy costs
2. **Rebate Optimization**: Structure CME trading to maximize rebates
3. **Market Data Costs**: Factor in CME market data fees
4. **Connectivity**: Consider co-location for low-latency arbitrage

### Example: Box Spread + Futures Hedge

**Scenario**: Box spread on SPX options + E-mini S&P 500 (ES) futures hedge

**Costs**:

- SPX options: Exchange fees + market data (CBOE)
- ES futures: Exchange fees + market data (CME)
- **Total**: Sum of both exchange costs
- **Optimization**: Maximize rebates on both exchanges

## Cost Analysis Framework

### Total Cost of Trading

**Total Cost = Exchange Fees - Rebates + Market Data + Connectivity + Clearing**

### Example Calculation

**Assumptions**:

- 10,000 ES contracts/month
- Exchange fee: $X.XX/contract
- Rebate: $Y.YY/contract (based on volume tier)
- Market data: $Z,ZZZ/month
- Connectivity: $A,AAA/month
- Clearing: $B.BB/contract

**Monthly Cost**:

```
Exchange Fees: 10,000 × $X.XX = $XX,XXX
Rebates:       10,000 × $Y.YY = -$YY,YYY
Market Data:                   $Z,ZZZ
Connectivity:                  $A,AAA
Clearing:      10,000 × $B.BB = $BB,BBB
───────────────────────────────────────
Total:                         $XX,XXX
```

### Break-Even Analysis

Calculate minimum volume required to:

- Qualify for rebate tiers
- Cover fixed costs (market data, connectivity)
- Achieve target net cost per contract
