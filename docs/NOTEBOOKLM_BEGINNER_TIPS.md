# NotebookLM Beginner Tips - Expert Guide

**Source**: [8 expert tips for getting started with NotebookLM](https://blog.google/technology/ai/notebooklm-beginner-tips/) - Google Blog, October 18, 2024

**Author**: Steven Johnson (NotebookLM Team Member & Author)

## Overview

NotebookLM is Google's AI-powered research assistant designed for deeper understanding of topics and materials. It acts as a virtual note-taking and research assistant that digests, analyzes, and synthesizes information from multiple sources.

### Key Features

- Create individual notebooks dedicated to topics or projects
- Upload up to **50 sources** with up to **25 million words**
- Supports PDFs, Google Docs, websites, YouTube videos
- Uses **Gemini 1.5's multimodal capabilities** to assess and connect sources
- Provides citations linking back to original passages
- **Private by design**: Your information is never shared or used to train the model

## 8 Expert Tips from Steven Johnson

### 1. Experiment with Your Recent Documents — Even If They're Random

**First Step for New Users**:

- Upload your **10 most recent documents** into one notebook
- Start experimenting by asking questions
- Even if documents are unrelated, it's a great way to test what NotebookLM can do
- Works whether you know the material well or it's new to you
- Helps uncover interesting insights

**Why This Works**: It's a low-pressure way to understand NotebookLM's capabilities without needing perfect organization.

### 2. Create One Main Notebook and Then Identify Topic-Based Notebooks

**The "Everything Notebook" Strategy**:

- Keep a main "everything notebook" with general knowledge you work with daily
- Include:
  - Inspirational quotes from books
  - Core company documents
  - Brainstorming ideas captured over years
  - Frequently referenced materials

**Topic-Specific Notebooks**:

- Also create topic- or project-based notebooks
- Example: A notebook exclusively for NotebookLM work containing:
  - Press releases
  - Feature descriptions
  - Internal documentation

- This creates a **personalized AI** that understands your specific context
- You can ask contextual questions like: *"Draft the outline of a blog post for that feature we were discussing last week"*

**Workflow**: Use the "everything notebook" for open-ended exploration, then create focused notebooks for specific projects or topics.

### 3. Use NotebookLM to Connect the Dots from Different Materials

**The Problem NotebookLM Solves**:

- Information scattered across desktop folders, browser tabs, and various locations
- Need to synthesize information from multiple sources

**Integration with Gemini/Gems**:

- Use as a **next step after using Gemini and Gems**
- After brainstorming or open-ended conversations in those tools:
  1. Save results to NotebookLM
  2. Add related articles, images, notes
  3. Let NotebookLM synthesize everything into one coherent format

**Example Workflow (Creating a Slide Deck)**:

1. Brainstorm with Gemini
2. Open relevant article tabs
3. Save design inspiration images
4. Add notes to a Google Doc
5. Feed everything into NotebookLM
6. Get synthesized, easy-to-digest format
7. Reference everything in one place when building presentation

### 4. Start with the Suggested Questions

**Don't Know What to Ask?**

- NotebookLM provides **suggested questions** to help you get started
- Similar to Gemini-powered side panel in Workspace tools
- The model helps you ask questions that guide you through the material
- Find starter questions in the **"Notebook Guide"** after uploading first sources
- Follow-up queries are suggested based on:
  - What you've already asked
  - What you've uploaded

**Tip**: Let the suggested questions guide you until you think of specific questions you want to ask.

### 5. Ask NotebookLM to Deliver Info in Different Ways

**Different Formats for Different Needs**:

- Not everyone processes information the same way
- NotebookLM can present information in various formats using **Notebook Guide** feature:
  - **FAQ** format
  - **Briefing document**
  - **Timeline**
  - **Table of contents**
  - **Study guide**
  - **Audio Overview** (new feature) - transforms information into an engaging conversation between two AI "hosts"

**Benefits**:

- See what works best for your learning style
- Present information to others in formats they prefer
- Different formats reveal different insights

### 6. Don't Shy Away from Creative Uses

**Beyond Work and School**:

- Excellent for work and school projects (NotebookLM Business now available)
- Great for creative projects:
  - **Writing fiction**: Fantasy and sci-fi novels
  - **Game development**: World building and intricate backstories
  - **Screenplays**: Character development and plot tracking

**Creative Example Workflow**:

- For a screenplay, you might have:
  - Notes in a doc
  - Saved webpages
  - Photos and videos as inspiration

- Put everything into a single notebook
- Ask questions like:
  - *"What was the deal with that one character?"*
  - *"Remind me which characters live where?"*
  - *"Which characters do you think are most compelling?"*
  - *"What are your favorite parts?"*

**Steven's Insight**: *"It's like being able to control F for 'interestingness'!"*

### 7. Turn Your Sources into Audio Overviews

**The Audio Overview Feature**:

- Turn every document you upload into an **Audio Overview**
- After a few minutes of generation, you get:
  - Entertaining audio conversation
  - Engaging discussion about your uploaded content
  - Two AI "hosts" discussing your material

**Customization (New Feature)**:

- Click **"Customize"** in Audio Overviews panel
- Enter short description of what you want hosts to focus on
- Adjust the approach and level of sophistication
- Example use case: Upload your own writing and ask for constructive criticism

**Steven's Recommendation**: Try this with the 10 documents you start with - it's mind-blowing!

### 8. Revisit Your NotebookLM Chat Sessions

**Don't Lose Your Insights**:

- Use **"save to note"** button in every text exchange to save interesting content
- At the end of a conversation, ask NotebookLM to:
  - Summarize key points from the conversation
  - Create a single note that goes in that notebook
  - Capture all progress and insights from your dialogue

**Steven's Workflow Example**:

1. Have a conversation about one of your notebooks
2. Ask questions and follow-up questions
3. Ask it to refine your ideas
4. At the end: *"Summarize the key points from this conversation into a single note"*
5. Future You will thank Past You for making it easy to pick up where you left off

**Why This Matters**: Chat sessions can contain valuable insights that might be lost if not captured. The summary note becomes a knowledge artifact you can reference later.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Start Simple**: Upload random recent documents to experiment
2. **Organize Gradually**: Build an "everything notebook" then create focused topic notebooks
3. **Synthesize Scattered Info**: Use NotebookLM to connect information from multiple sources
4. **Use Suggestions**: Start with suggested questions when stuck
5. **Try Different Formats**: Present information in various ways (FAQ, timeline, audio, etc.)
6. **Be Creative**: Use NotebookLM for creative projects, not just work
7. **Audio Overviews**: Transform your sources into engaging audio conversations
8. **Save Conversations**: Summarize chat sessions into notes for future reference

## Best Practices Summary

- **Organize by workflow**: Everything notebook → Topic notebooks → Project notebooks
- **Integrate with other tools**: Use after Gemini/Gems brainstorming sessions
- **Experiment with formats**: Find what works for your learning and presentation needs
- **Think creatively**: NotebookLM isn't just for traditional research
- **Preserve insights**: Always save or summarize valuable conversations
